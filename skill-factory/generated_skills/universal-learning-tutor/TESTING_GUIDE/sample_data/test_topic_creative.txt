Sample Topic Request - Creative Subject

Topic: Jazz Piano Improvisation

User Profile:
- Background: Classical piano training (10 years), can read music well
- Current Knowledge Level: Beginner in jazz (understand basic theory)
- Learning Goal: Improvise solos over jazz standards
- Time Available: 5 hours per week (including practice time)
- Preferred Format: Both visual and audio (audio for listening practice, visual for theory)

Specific Interests:
- Chord voicings and comping
- Scale choices for improvisation
- Rhythmic vocabulary and phrasing
- Bebop language and licks
- Listening and transcription skills

Expected Challenge Areas:
- Thinking in chord tones vs. scales
- Playing "in the pocket" rhythmically
- Developing unique voice vs. copying licks
- Connecting theory to ear training

Success Criteria:
- Improvise confidently on "Autumn Leaves" and similar standards
- Comp behind other soloists
- Understand what great players are doing
- Develop beginning of personal style

Additional Context:
- Strong classical technique but stiff rhythmically
- Need to develop jazz ear (currently too theory-dependent)
- Want to balance structured practice with creative exploration
- Interested in bebop and post-bop styles (Bird, Bud Powell, Bill Evans)
