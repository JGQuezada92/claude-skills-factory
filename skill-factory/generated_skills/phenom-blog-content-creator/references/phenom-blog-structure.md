# Phenom Blog Structure and Style Guide

This document outlines the structure, formatting, and stylistic patterns used in Phenom blog posts, extracted from analysis of published content.

## Overall Blog Structure

### 1. Introduction Section
- **Hook**: Begin with a compelling question or statement that addresses a pressing business challenge
- **Context**: Set the stage with current industry trends or challenges
- **Event/Program Reference**: Reference relevant Phenom events, programs, or initiatives (e.g., "During this year's Phenom AI Day...")
- **Narrative Bridge**: Connect the hook to the content that follows (e.g., "What emerged is a compelling narrative about...")
- **Call-to-Action Link**: Include a prominent link to related content or events (e.g., "Watch all of AI Day 2024 on demand!")

### 2. Table of Contents Section
- **Section Header**: "## In This Article"
- **Format**: Bulleted list with anchor links to each major section
- **Purpose**: Provide navigation for readers and improve SEO

### 3. Main Content Sections (Case Studies/Stories)

Each case study or main content section follows this pattern:

#### Section Header
- **Format**: `## [Company/Organization Name]`
- **Style**: Company name as main heading, linked to full case study when available

#### Opening Paragraph
- **Company Context**: Brief description of the company and its mission/industry
- **Challenge Statement**: Quote from company representative describing their challenge
- **Format**: Use quoted text from company representatives with attribution
- **Example Pattern**: "[Company Name], which [what they do], faces [challenge type]: '[quote from representative]'"

#### Strategy/Approach Section
- **Header**: Optional subheader (e.g., "### **Tackling the Speed vs. Quality Challenge**")
- **Numbered Strategy Points**: When applicable, present strategy as numbered list
- **Format**: Each point includes a bold label followed by explanation
- **Example**: "1. **Targeted Messaging:** [explanation]"

#### Implementation Details Section
- **Header**: "### **Innovative Solutions Driving Results**" or similar
- **Bold Feature/Tool Names**: Highlight specific tools, features, or solutions
- **Format**: Bold feature name followed by colon, then description
- **Example**: "**AI-powered Chatbot:** [description]"
- **Include Links**: Link to relevant resources, tools, or case studies

#### Results Section
- **Introduction Phrase**: "The impact of these changes..." or "The impact of their efforts? Pretty amazing:"
- **Format**: Bulleted list with quantifiable metrics
- **Metric Formatting**:
  - Use specific numbers and percentages
  - Include timeframes where relevant
  - Show before/after comparisons when applicable
  - Example: "Net Promoter Score increased from 64 to 78"
  - Example: "295% increase in job applications"
  - Example: "Time to fill reduced from 72 days to 59 days"

#### Related Links Section
- **Format**: "**Related:** [**Link Text**](url)"
- **Placement**: After results, before closing paragraph

#### Closing Paragraph
- **Forward-Looking Statement**: Quote or statement about future plans or continuous improvement
- **Mission Connection**: Tie back to company mission or broader goals
- **Format**: Include closing quote from company representative

#### Visual Elements
- **YouTube Embeds**: Place video embeds after case study content
- **Images**: Company logos, headshots, or product images
- **Dividers**: Use `* * *` for section breaks

### 4. Conclusion Section
- **Call-to-Action**: Link to related content or case studies
- **Example**: "To see how other companies are benefitting from AI, [check out all of our customer stories](url)!"

### 5. Author Attribution
- **Format**: Author headshot image, author name, date
- **Topics**: Include topic tags (e.g., "Topics: Customer Stories")

### 6. Related Content Section
- **Header**: "#### Related"
- **Format**: Image thumbnails with linked article titles
- **Count**: Typically 3 related articles

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone
- **Professional but Approachable**: Use professional language while remaining accessible
- **Data-Driven**: Emphasize quantifiable results and metrics
- **Outcome-Focused**: Highlight real-world impact and business outcomes
- **Storytelling**: Use narrative elements to engage readers

### Voice
- **Third-Person**: Write in third person (avoid "you" unless in quotes)
- **Active Voice**: Prefer active voice over passive
- **Direct Quotes**: Use quotes from company representatives liberally
- **Conversational Elements**: Include phrases like "Pretty amazing:" for emphasis

### Language Patterns
- **Challenge Statements**: "faces a significant hurdle", "struggling with", "managing more efficient"
- **Solution Introductions**: "To tackle this challenge head-on", "Their journey began with"
- **Result Introductions**: "The impact of these changes", "The impact of their efforts?"
- **Transition Phrases**: "Among them", "What emerged", "Moving forward"

### Emphasis Techniques
- **Bold for Features/Tools**: Bold feature names, tool names, and key concepts
- **Italics for Emphasis**: Use italics for emphasis within quotes or descriptions
- **Quotes for Authenticity**: Include direct quotes from company representatives
- **Numbers for Credibility**: Always include specific metrics and percentages

## Formatting Patterns

### Headers
- **H1**: Main blog title (# Title)
- **H2**: Main sections (## Section Name)
- **H3**: Subsections (### **Subsection Name**)
- **Bold in H3**: Subsection headers often use bold formatting

### Links
- **Format**: `[Link Text](url)`
- **Placement**: 
  - Company names link to full case studies
  - Feature/tool names link to product pages
  - Related content links placed strategically
- **Style**: Bold for prominent links (e.g., `[**Link Text**](url)`)

### Lists
- **Numbered Lists**: For sequential strategies or steps
- **Bulleted Lists**: For results, features, or non-sequential items
- **Bold List Items**: Use bold for key terms in list items (e.g., "**Efficiently screen** high volumes")

### Visual Breaks
- **YouTube Embeds**: Place after main content sections
- **Images**: Author headshots, company logos, product images
- **Dividers**: `* * *` for major section breaks
- **Horizontal Rules**: Use sparingly for emphasis

### Metrics Formatting
- **Before/After**: "reduced from X to Y"
- **Percentage Changes**: "X% increase/decrease"
- **Timeframes**: "over 90 days", "in under 5 months", "every month in 2024"
- **Compound Metrics**: "X new coworkers hired through [method]"

## Content Flow Patterns

### Opening Flow
1. Hook question/statement
2. Context setting
3. Event/program reference
4. Narrative bridge
5. CTA link

### Case Study Flow
1. Company name and context
2. Challenge/problem (with quote)
3. Strategy/approach (numbered or bulleted)
4. Implementation details (bold features)
5. Results (bulleted metrics)
6. Related links
7. Closing statement/quote

### Transition Between Sections
- Use company names as section headers
- Each section is self-contained but builds on the overall narrative
- Use visual breaks (videos, images) between major sections

## Key Elements to Always Include

1. **Quantifiable Metrics**: Every case study must include specific, measurable results
2. **Direct Quotes**: Include quotes from company representatives
3. **Related Links**: Link to full case studies, resources, or related content
4. **Visual Elements**: Include appropriate images, videos, or embeds
5. **Forward-Looking Statements**: End sections with quotes or statements about future plans
6. **Mission Connection**: Tie results back to company mission or broader goals

## Content Quality Standards

- **Professional Quality**: Production-ready content suitable for publication
- **Data-Driven**: All claims supported by specific metrics
- **Authentic**: Use real quotes and real company examples
- **Actionable**: Provide insights readers can apply
- **Engaging**: Use storytelling and narrative elements
- **Comprehensive**: Cover the topic thoroughly with multiple examples
