# Management Consulting Frameworks

## Strategic Analysis Frameworks

### SWOT Analysis

**When to Use**: Competitive positioning, strategic assessment

**Components**:
- **Strengths**: Internal advantages, capabilities
- **Weaknesses**: Internal limitations, gaps
- **Opportunities**: External market opportunities
- **Threats**: External risks, competitive threats

### PESTEL Analysis

**When to Use**: Macro-environmental analysis, strategic planning

**Components**:
- **Political**: Government policies, regulations
- **Economic**: Economic conditions, market trends
- **Social**: Demographic trends, workforce shifts
- **Technological**: Technology trends, innovation
- **Environmental**: Sustainability, ESG factors
- **Legal**: Regulatory requirements, compliance

### Value Chain Analysis

**When to Use**: Process optimization, competitive advantage identification

**HCM Value Chain**:
- Primary activities: Product development, sales, implementation, support
- Support activities: HR, technology, procurement, infrastructure

## Process Improvement Methodologies

### Six Sigma

**When to Use**: Quality improvement, defect reduction

**DMAIC Process**:
- **Define**: Define problem and goals
- **Measure**: Measure current performance
- **Analyze**: Analyze root causes
- **Improve**: Implement improvements
- **Control**: Sustain improvements

### Lean Methodology

**When to Use**: Waste reduction, efficiency improvement

**Key Principles**:
- Identify value from customer perspective
- Map value stream
- Create flow
- Establish pull
- Pursue perfection

**Waste Types**:
- Overproduction, waiting, transportation, over-processing, inventory, motion, defects

### Kaizen

**When to Use**: Continuous improvement, incremental changes

**Approach**:
- Small, incremental improvements
- Employee involvement
- Continuous monitoring and adjustment

## Change Management Frameworks

### ADKAR Model

**When to Use**: Organizational change, implementation

**Components**:
- **Awareness**: Need for change
- **Desire**: Willingness to participate
- **Knowledge**: How to change
- **Ability**: Skills to change
- **Reinforcement**: Sustain change

### Kotter's 8-Step Change Model

**When to Use**: Major organizational transformations

**Steps**:
1. Create urgency
2. Build coalition
3. Form vision
4. Communicate vision
5. Remove obstacles
6. Create short-term wins
7. Build on change
8. Anchor change

## Operational Excellence Frameworks

### Process Mapping

**When to Use**: Current state analysis, process optimization

**Steps**:
1. Map current process
2. Identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies
3. Design future state
4. Implement improvements
5. Monitor and refine

### Benchmarking

**When to Use**: Performance comparison, best practice identification

**Types**:
- **Internal**: Compare across departments/regions
- **Competitive**: Compare to competitors
- **Functional**: Compare to best-in-class
- **Generic**: Compare across industries

## Strategic Planning Frameworks

### McKinsey 3 Horizons

**When to Use**: Growth planning, portfolio management

**Horizons**:
- **Horizon 1**: Core business, current operations
- **Horizon 2**: Emerging opportunities, adjacent markets
- **Horizon 3**: Future options, long-term bets

### BCG Growth-Share Matrix

**When to Use**: Product portfolio analysis, resource allocation

**Quadrants**:
- **Stars**: High growth, high share (invest)
- **Cash Cows**: Low growth, high share (harvest)
- **Question Marks**: High growth, low share (evaluate)
- **Dogs**: Low growth, low share (divest)

## Organizational Design Frameworks

### GTM Structure Design

**When to Use**: Sales organization design, territory planning

**Considerations**:
- Geographic vs. vertical vs. account-based
- Inside sales vs. field sales mix
- Sales:SE:CSM ratios
- Territory sizing and account assignment

### Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

**When to Use**: Product-market fit, customer understanding

**Approach**:
- Identify customer jobs to be done
- Understand functional, emotional, social jobs
- Design solutions that complete jobs better

## Process Optimization for HCM

### Recruiting Process Optimization

**Key Metrics**:
- Time-to-fill
- Cost-per-hire
- Quality of hire
- Candidate experience

**Best Practices**:
- Automated screening
- Structured interviews
- Clear job descriptions
- Efficient interview scheduling

### Implementation Process Optimization

**Key Metrics**:
- Time-to-value
- Implementation success rate
- Customer satisfaction
- Resource utilization

**Best Practices**:
- Phased rollouts
- Change management
- Training programs
- Ongoing support

### Sales Process Optimization

**Key Metrics**:
- Sales cycle length
- Win rate
- Quota attainment
- Deal velocity

**Best Practices**:
- Sales methodology (MEDDIC, BANT, etc.)
- Sales enablement
- Pipeline management
- Competitive positioning
