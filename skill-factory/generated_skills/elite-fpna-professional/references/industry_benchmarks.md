# Industry Benchmarks Guide
## Financial Benchmarks by Industry Sector

This guide provides typical financial benchmarks across major industries to support comparative analysis and performance assessment.

---

## How to Use Benchmarks

**Important Considerations:**
- Benchmarks represent typical ranges, not absolute targets
- Company size, stage, and business model create variations
- Public company data provides most reliable benchmarks
- Private company benchmarks are estimates based on available data
- Always consider multiple benchmarks, not single data points

---

## Technology / Software / SaaS

### Growth Metrics
- **Revenue Growth:** 30-50% (high-growth), 15-30% (mature)
- **ARR Growth:** 40-60% (early stage), 20-40% (scale stage)
- **Customer Growth:** 30-50%

### Profitability Metrics
- **Gross Margin:** 70-85%
- **Operating Margin:** -10% to +20% (varies by growth investment)
- **EBITDA Margin:** 5-25%
- **Net Margin:** 0-20%
- **Rule of 40:** >40% (Revenue Growth % + EBITDA Margin %)

### Efficiency Metrics
- **CAC Payback:** 12-18 months
- **LTV/CAC Ratio:** >3x
- **Magic Number:** >0.75
- **Sales Efficiency:** 1.0-1.5x
- **R&D as % Revenue:** 15-25%
- **S&M as % Revenue:** 30-50%

### Retention Metrics
- **Gross Revenue Retention:** >90%
- **Net Revenue Retention:** 110-130%
- **Logo Churn:** <10% annually

### Unit Economics
- **ARPU:** Growing 10-20% annually
- **Revenue per Employee:** $200K-$400K

## Company Size Considerations

### Early-Stage (<$10M Revenue)
- **Growth Priority:** 50-100% growth typical
- **Profitability:** Often negative (investing in growth)
- **Burn Rate:** 12+ months runway critical
- **Efficiency:** Focus on unit economics, not absolute profits

### Growth-Stage ($10M-$100M)
- **Growth:** 30-60%
- **Path to Profitability:** Improving EBITDA margins
- **Efficiency:** Scaling efficiently (Rule of 40 for SaaS)
- **Working Capital:** Tighter management needed

### Mid-Market ($100M-$1B)
- **Growth:** 15-30%
- **Profitability:** Positive EBITDA and net income expected
- **Efficiency:** Operational excellence focus
- **Returns:** ROE, ROIC benchmarks matter

### Large Enterprise ($1B+)
- **Growth:** 5-15%
- **Profitability:** Consistent margins expected
- **Efficiency:** Optimize at scale
- **Returns:** Capital allocation excellence
- **Capital Markets:** Dividends/buybacks considered

## How to Apply Benchmarks

### 1. Select Appropriate Benchmarks
- Match industry sector
- Consider company size/stage
- Account for business model differences
- Use multiple sources

### 2. Calculate Gaps
- Identify where company exceeds benchmarks
- Identify where company lags
- Quantify performance gaps

### 3. Investigate Variances
- Understand why gaps exist
- Determine if gaps are strategic or operational
- Assess sustainability of outperformance

### 4. Set Targets
- Use benchmarks to inform targets
- Consider industry position (leader vs. follower)
- Set realistic improvement goals

### 5. Track Over Time
- Monitor progress toward benchmarks
- Update benchmarks as industry evolves
- Adjust targets as needed

## Benchmark Sources

### Public Data
- **SEC Filings (10-K, 10-Q):** Most reliable for public companies
- **Industry Reports:** IBISWorld, Gartner, Forrester
- **Trade Associations:** Industry-specific associations
- **Financial Data Providers:** Capital IQ, Bloomberg, FactSet

### Private Company Data
- **Private Equity Portfolios:** Limited but valuable
- **Venture Capital Reports:** State of SaaS, etc.
- **Industry Surveys:** Various sources by industry
- **Consulting Firm Reports:** McKinsey, Bain, BCG reports

---

*Remember: Benchmarks provide context, not absolute targets. Company strategy, competitive positioning, and stage drive appropriate performance levels.*
