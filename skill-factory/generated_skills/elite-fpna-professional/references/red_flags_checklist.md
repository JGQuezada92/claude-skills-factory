# Financial Red Flags Checklist
## Comprehensive Guide to Identifying Financial Warning Signs

This checklist provides systematic framework for identifying financial red flags across revenue, profitability, cash flow, balance sheet, and operational metrics.

---

**Severity Levels:**
- 游댮 **CRITICAL:** Immediate attention required, potential going concern issues

---

## Revenue Red Flags

### 游댮 CRITICAL

**Declining Revenue (>20% YoY)**
- **What it Signals:** Severe business deterioration, market share loss, or product obsolescence
- **What to Investigate:** Customer churn, competitive dynamics, market changes, product issues
- **Potential Impact:** Business viability concerns

**Revenue Recognition Irregularities**
- **What it Signals:** Aggressive or fraudulent accounting
- **What to Investigate:** Channel stuffing, premature revenue recognition, side agreements
- **Potential Impact:** Financial restatements, regulatory issues

### 游 HIGH

**High Customer Concentration (>50% from top 10)**
- **What it Signals:** Extreme dependence on few customers, revenue volatility risk
- **What to Investigate:** Customer relationship health, contract terms, alternatives
- **Potential Impact:** Revenue cliff if major customer leaves

**Decelerating Growth Rates**
- **What it Signals:** Market saturation, competitive pressure, or execution issues
- **What to Investigate:** Market dynamics, competitive wins/losses, sales productivity
- **Potential Impact:** Valuation compression, missed targets

**Rising Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)**
- **What it Signals:** Collection problems, customer credit issues, or aggressive revenue recognition
- **What to Investigate:** Aging receivables, customer creditworthiness, payment terms
- **Potential Impact:** Cash flow deterioration, bad debt write-offs

### 游리 MEDIUM

**Revenue Seasonality Changes**
- **What it Signals:** Business model shifts or execution issues
- **What to Investigate:** Product mix changes, customer behavior, market dynamics
- **Potential Impact:** Forecasting difficulties, working capital challenges

**Geographic Concentration Risk**
- **What it Signals:** Exposure to regional economic or regulatory risks
- **What to Investigate:** Diversification opportunities, regional dependencies
- **Potential Impact:** Revenue volatility from regional issues

**High Customer Churn (>20% annually)**
- **What it Signals:** Product-market fit issues, service problems, or competitive weakness
- **What to Investigate:** Customer satisfaction, competitive positioning, retention initiatives
- **Potential Impact:** Slowing or negative growth

## Margin & Profitability Red Flags

### 游댮 CRITICAL

**Negative Gross Margins**
- **What it Signals:** Fundamental business model problem
- **What to Investigate:** Pricing strategy, cost structure, competitive dynamics
- **Potential Impact:** Unsustainable business

**Gross Margin Compression (>500bp YoY)**
- **What it Signals:** Severe pricing pressure, cost inflation, or adverse mix shift
- **What to Investigate:** Pricing actions, input costs, product mix trends
- **Potential Impact:** Profitability collapse

### 游 HIGH

**Declining Gross Margins (>200bp YoY)**
- **What it Signals:** Pricing pressure, cost inflation, or unfavorable mix
- **What to Investigate:** Pricing power, cost trends, mix dynamics
- **Potential Impact:** Compressed operating margins and profitability

**Operating Margin Below Industry**
- **What it Signals:** Operational inefficiency or cost structure issues
- **What to Investigate:** Cost benchmarking, efficiency opportunities, overhead levels
- **Potential Impact:** Competitive disadvantage, valuation discount

**Inconsistent Profitability**
- **What it Signals:** Volatile business model or poor cost management
- **What to Investigate:** Revenue volatility, fixed cost structure, operational issues
- **Potential Impact:** Forecasting difficulty, financing challenges

### 游리 MEDIUM

**Declining Operating Leverage**
- **What it Signals:** Costs growing faster than revenue
- **What to Investigate:** Cost discipline, efficiency initiatives, scaling issues
- **Potential Impact:** Margin compression

**High Dependence on One-Time Gains**
- **What it Signals:** Core business underperformance masked by unusual items
- **What to Investigate:** Normalized profitability, sustainability of gains
- **Potential Impact:** Profit disappointment when one-time items cease

## Cash Flow Red Flags

### 游댮 CRITICAL

**Negative Operating Cash Flow (Multiple Periods)**
- **What it Signals:** Business consuming cash, not generating it
- **What to Investigate:** Burn rate, path to positive cash flow, funding needs
- **Potential Impact:** Liquidity crisis, need for capital raise

**Cash Flow << Net Income (Ratio <0.7)**
- **What it Signals:** Poor earnings quality, aggressive accounting, or working capital issues
- **What to Investigate:** Accruals, revenue recognition timing, working capital trends
- **Potential Impact:** Overstatement of profitability

**Rapidly Declining Cash Balance**
- **What it Signals:** Liquidity concerns, potential funding crisis
- **What to Investigate:** Burn rate, cash runway, financing options
- **Potential Impact:** Going concern issues

### 游 HIGH

**Deteriorating Working Capital Trends**
- **What it Signals:** Operational issues or collection/payment problems
- **What to Investigate:** DSO, DIO, DPO trends, working capital management
- **Potential Impact:** Cash flow squeeze

**High Burn Rate vs. Runway**
- **What it Signals:** Limited time to reach profitability or raise capital
- **What to Investigate:** Path to profitability, capital requirements, funding options
- **Potential Impact:** Forced dilutive financing or restructuring

**Free Cash Flow Negative**
- **What it Signals:** Business consuming more cash than generating
- **What to Investigate:** CapEx needs, operating cash flow issues, growth investments
- **Potential Impact:** Funding needs

### 游리 MEDIUM

**Cash Conversion Cycle Lengthening**
- **What it Signals:** Working capital becoming less efficient
- **What to Investigate:** Collection, inventory management, payment terms
- **Potential Impact:** Cash flow pressure

**Rising CapEx Requirements**
- **What it Signals:** Increasing capital intensity
- **What to Investigate:** Maintenance vs. growth CapEx, ROI on investments
- **Potential Impact:** Lower free cash flow

## Balance Sheet Red Flags

### 游댮 CRITICAL

**Negative Equity**
- **What it Signals:** Accumulated losses exceed invested capital
- **What to Investigate:** Path to positive equity, restructuring needs
- **Potential Impact:** Bankruptcy risk, covenant violations

**Excessive Leverage (Debt/EBITDA >5x)**
- **What it Signals:** High financial risk, limited flexibility
- **What to Investigate:** Debt structure, refinancing risks, cash flow adequacy
- **Potential Impact:** Default risk, forced asset sales

**Current Ratio <1.0**
- **What it Signals:** Insufficient current assets to cover current liabilities
- **What to Investigate:** Liquidity sources, refinancing needs, asset sales
- **Potential Impact:** Liquidity crisis

**Covenant Violations**
- **What it Signals:** Financial distress, breach of lending agreements
- **What to Investigate:** Waiver negotiations, covenant amendments, restructuring
- **Potential Impact:** Loan acceleration, default

### 游 HIGH

**Declining Current Ratio**
- **What it Signals:** Deteriorating liquidity position
- **What to Investigate:** Working capital trends, debt maturities, cash generation
- **Potential Impact:** Liquidity pressure

**High Debt Maturities**
- **What it Signals:** Refinancing risk
- **What to Investigate:** Refinancing plans, cash flow to service debt, alternative financing
- **Potential Impact:** Refinancing challenges, higher interest costs

**Low Interest Coverage (<2x)**
- **What it Signals:** Limited ability to service debt from operations
- **What to Investigate:** Operating improvement opportunities, debt restructuring options
- **Potential Impact:** Default risk if operations deteriorate

**Rising Debt Levels**
- **What it Signals:** Increasing leverage and financial risk
- **What to Investigate:** Use of proceeds, debt service capability, deleveraging plan
- **Potential Impact:** Reduced financial flexibility

### 游리 MEDIUM

**Declining Asset Turnover**
- **What it Signals:** Less efficient asset utilization
- **What to Investigate:** Asset productivity, capacity utilization, divestiture opportunities
- **Potential Impact:** Lower returns on capital

**Inventory Buildup**
- **What it Signals:** Slowing sales or poor inventory management
- **What to Investigate:** Obsolescence risk, demand trends, inventory turns
- **Potential Impact:** Write-downs, cash tied up in inventory

- **What it Signals:** Cost discipline issues or operating deleverage
- **What to Investigate:** Cost benchmarking, efficiency initiatives, scaling issues
- **Potential Impact:** Profitability pressure

**High Employee Turnover**

- **What to Investigate:** Retention initiatives, compensation benchmarking, culture assessment
- **Potential Impact:** Execution risk, lost institutional knowledge

### 游리 MEDIUM

**Declining Sales Productivity**
- **What it Signals:** Sales execution issues or market headwinds
- **What to Investigate:** Sales process, training, compensation, market dynamics
- **Potential Impact:** Revenue growth challenges

**Increasing Cost per Unit**
- **What it Signals:** Inefficiency or input cost inflation
- **What to Investigate:** Process improvement, procurement optimization, pricing

## Quality of Earnings Red Flags

### 游댮 CRITICAL

**Frequent Restatements**
- **What it Signals:** Accounting control issues or manipulation
- **What to Investigate:** Root causes, control improvements, management integrity
- **Potential Impact:** Loss of credibility, regulatory scrutiny

**Audit Qualifications or Going Concern Warnings**
- **What it Signals:** Serious financial or operational issues
- **What to Investigate:** Viability assessment, restructuring needs, stakeholder communications
- **Potential Impact:** Financing difficulties, potential failure

### 游 HIGH

**High Non-Cash Charges**
- **What it Signals:** Earnings quality concerns
- **What to Investigate:** Nature of charges, cash flow generation, sustainability
- **Potential Impact:** Overstatement of profitability

**Frequent "One-Time" Items**
- **What it Signals:** Recurring items labeled as non-recurring
- **What to Investigate:** True normalized earnings, sustainability
- **Potential Impact:** Misrepresentation of core performance

**Aggressive Revenue Recognition**
- **What it Signals:** Potential overstatement of revenue
- **What to Investigate:** Revenue recognition policies, contract terms, ASC 606 compliance
- **Potential Impact:** Revenue restatements

**Rising Accounts Receivable > Revenue Growth**
- **What it Signals:** Aggressive revenue recognition or collection issues
- **What to Investigate:** DSO trends, aging schedules, credit policies
- **Potential Impact:** Bad debt write-offs, revenue quality concerns

### 游리 MEDIUM

**High Related Party Transactions**
- **What it Signals:** Potential conflicts of interest or non-arm's length dealings
- **What to Investigate:** Transaction terms, market rates, governance
- **Potential Impact:** Value transfer, governance concerns

## Red Flag Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify
Use this checklist to systematically identify potential red flags in financial statements and operations.

### Step 2: Investigate
For each red flag identified:
- Gather additional data
- Interview management
- Benchmark against peers
- Analyze trends

### Step 3: Assess Severity
- **CRITICAL:** Immediate action required
- **HIGH:** Prompt corrective action needed
- **MEDIUM:** Develop action plan
- **LOW:** Monitor and track

### Step 4: Recommend Actions
- Root cause remediation
- Process improvements
- Strategic adjustments
- Monitoring protocols

### Step 5: Monitor
- Track metrics over time
- Assess effectiveness of actions
- Update red flag assessment

## Common Red Flag Combinations

Certain combinations of red flags are particularly concerning:

**"Death Spiral" Pattern:**
- Declining revenue
- Negative operating cash flow
- Rising leverage
- Management turnover
- **Action:** Immediate turnaround or restructuring

**"Aggressive Accounting" Pattern:**
- Revenue growing faster than receivables
- Cash flow << net income
- Frequent one-time items
- High accruals
- **Action:** Deep dive into accounting quality

**"Zombie Company" Pattern:**
- Stagnant revenue
- Low/negative margins
- High debt
- Minimal cash generation
- **Action:** Strategic alternatives assessment

---

*Use this checklist as a systematic framework. Context matters - not all red flags indicate serious problems, but they warrant investigation and explanation.*
